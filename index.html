About the Theory

The Scheerer Theory proposes that mass is not fundamental, but emerges from the compression of a quantum wavefunction. The core equation derived from this framework is m = E / A², where mass arises from energy (E) confined within a spatial amplitude (A). This theory explains the onset of relativistic behavior, refraction, photon mass in confinement, and other phenomena as consequences of localization and amplitude reduction.
